Pre Conference Technical Programs of SASI-ITE'24
A Tutorial on

Distributed Strategies for Nonlinear System Identification

This tutorial explores the sophisticated realm of spatio-temporally coupled nonlinear parameter estimation, employing an innovative approach based on the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) and a distributed computational strategy. The theme revolves around empowering researchers, engineers, and data scientists with the knowledge and tools necessary to tackle complex nonlinear models in a distributed computing environment, considering both spatial and temporal dependencies.



Objective of Tutorial
(1) Acquire a deep understanding of nonlinear parameter estimation in spatio-temporally coupled systems.
(2) Comprehend the theoretical foundations of distributed estimation
(3) Develop proficiency in implementing distributed strategies for nonlinear parameter estimation.
(4) Apply the acquired knowledge to real-world case studies, demonstrating the effectiveness of the presented strategies.

Eligibility
Faculty members in all disciplines of Engineering & Sciences, Post Doctoral Fellows, Research Scholars, PG students, UG students who have an aptitude to work in Communication/Signal processing/deep learning/machine learning.
